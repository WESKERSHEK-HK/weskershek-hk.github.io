Carbon Neutrality PACMAN, an educational game exhibit that I had the privilege of developing for the Macau Science Museum. As the full stack developer behind this project, my role encompassed designing and building every aspect of the game, from the user interface to the gameplay system and even the content management backend.

The objective of Carbon Neutrality PACMAN was to educate children about carbon emissions in a fun and interactive manner. Through this game, museum visitors were able to embark on an exciting journey where they navigated through mazes, collecting points while learning about the impact of their choices on the environment. By engineering the entire game from front-end to back-end, I ensured that the experience was not only entertaining but also enriched with valuable educational content.

Thanks to the captivating gameplay and the immersive learning experience, Carbon Neutrality PACMAN attracted over 5000 children to the exhibit. It was truly rewarding to witness the joy and enthusiasm on their faces as they engaged with the game and absorbed important lessons about carbon neutrality. This project not only showcased my expertise in full stack development, but also contributed to the mission of promoting environmental awareness and sustainability among the younger generation.
