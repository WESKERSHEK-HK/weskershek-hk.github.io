  • Role

    Particle system

    TouchDesigner

For the WIND-OW project, I took on the role of the artist and created an interactive exhibit for an extreme weather exhibition held in TAIKWUN HK. The goal of the project was to visually represent the power and impact of typhoons in Hong Kong. Utilizing my skills in data visualization, particle systems, TouchDesigner, and Python, I developed a unique artwork that engaged and captivated the audience.

By collecting typhoon data from the Hong Kong Observatory, I wrote Python code to generate a dynamic particle system within TouchDesigner. The particles visually represented the movement and intensity of the typhoons, creating a mesmerizing and immersive experience for the viewers.

One of the key features of the exhibit was the interactive aspect. When audiences approached the artwork, the particle system would respond and interact with their presence, almost as if the wind was blowing them directly inside the screen. This interactive element added a layer of engagement and personal connection to the artwork, leaving a lasting impression on the visitors.

The WIND-OW exhibit was a resounding success, attracting more than 1000 visitors to experience and appreciate the power of extreme weather through the medium of art. The combination of data visualization, particle systems, and interactive design created a unique andcaptivating experience that resonated with the audience. Through this project, I was able to showcase the potential of technology and art to convey complex concepts and evoke emotions. The WIND-OW exhibit not only entertained and engaged visitors but also raised awareness about the impact of typhoons in Hong Kong, making it a valuable addition to the extreme weather exhibition held at TAIKWUN HK.
