England 1984, a generative graphic series.

In this series of AI-generated artworks, I sought to explore the parallels between modern-day surveillance culture and the dystopian world depicted in George Orwell’s classic novel 1984. London served as my inspiration, as its extensive network of CCTV cameras and mass surveillance programs continue to push the boundaries of privacy.

I used Stable Diffusion, an AI tool for image generation, to visualize my concepts. I fed the model text prompts that combined descriptions of London street scenes with passages and themes from 1984, such as Big Brother, thoughtcrime, and the ever-watchful eyes of the Party. The resulting surreal images fuse reality with fiction in unsettling ways.

Orwell’s dystopian vision of a totalitarian state that monitors and manipulates its citizens through excessive surveillance seems increasingly prescient. As technology advances, it becomes ever easier for governments and corporations to track our movements, behaviors and online activities. My artworks aim to start a conversation about protecting individual freedoms and reining in uncontrolled data collection in the digital age.

By merging my prompts about London with the compelling symbolism of 1984, I crafted a series of unsettling allegories about living under constant watch. Through an AI lens focused on privacy, control and the blurring lines between public and private, I invite viewers to reflect on surveillance issues in our society and consider how to avoid sliding towards an Orwellian future.
